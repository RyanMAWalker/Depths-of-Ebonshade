Character Creation

Guidance

As part of the campaign we introduced an additional ability for each race, known as "Stress" throughout the campaign your characters will be affected by different circumstances and situations which will have a positive or negative impact, this is guaged by thier stress level. As such we recommend using one of the races listed below or for your DM to balance accordingly. All races are balance around the D&D 5e free to use basic ruleset which can be found by clicking this link.

The classes below correspond to what is avaliable as free content from the D&D 5e as part of the free player's handbook and basic rules set. To access a full description of each class please click this link. or refer to the class section of the campaign.

Races

Dragonborn

A proud loyal race born of dragons, they look much like dragons in humanoid form standing close to 6 1/2 feet tall, the only draconic features laking are the wings and tail. To any Dragonborn, the clan is more important than life itself. Each Dragonborn's conduct reflects on the honor of his or her clan leading to their continual drive for self-improvement, valuing skill and excellence in all endeavors.

  • Ability Score Increase: Strength +2 Charisma +1
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 30ft.
  • Size: 6 to 7ft tall classed as medium.
  • Breath Weapon: You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. Your draconic ancestry determines size shape and damage type of exhalation. Each creature in the area of exhalation must make a saving throw.
  • Draconic ProtectionYou have a resistance to the damage type associated with your ancestry.
  • Strss Gain Average
  • Medium Stress: -1 Charisma +1 Performance
  • High Stress: -2 Charisma -2 Perception + 2 strength
  • High Stress actions: Will focus on protecting what they deem to be the highest valued member of the group roll at an advantage any action towards that member. Roll disadvantage on actions aiding all other members. Breath weapon radius increased by 5 ft, damage increased to 2d8.

Dwarf

Bold and hardy dwarves are known as skilled warriors, miners, and forgers. Standing well under 5ft tall, dwarves are broad and compact yet can weigh the same as a human 2ft taller. Dwarves are slow to trust and their grudges are legendary, they can live to be more than 400 years olds and will remember any wrongs cast against them during that time.

  • Ability score increase: Constitution +2
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 25ft
  • Size: 4 to 5ft tall classed as medium sized.
  • Dark Vision: Accustomed to life underground you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions, you can see in dim light within 60ft as if it was clear as day and in darkness as if it was dim.
  • Ironblood: Gain advantage on saving throws against poison and resistance against poison damage.
  • Stonecunning: Upon making a history check related to stonework you are considered proficient in the History skill and add double proficiency to your bonus.
  • Stress Gain: Low
  • Medium Stress: -1 Dexterity +2 Intimidation
  • High Stress: -2 Dexterity -1 Slight of Hand +2 Constitution
  • Stress Action: Their reactions start to shut down as they rely more on their dense frame. A high-stress dwarf can become an unbreakable immovable object when under attack. Enemies cannot roll at advantage when attacking. The dwarf cannot become stunned.

Elf

A magical people of otherworldly grace, with their unearthly grace and fine features, elves appear hauntingly beautiful to many other races. Elves can live for well over 700 years resulting in them remaining unfazed by petty troubles. They are more likely to be amused than excited and curious than greedy. Elves take up adventures out of wanderlust due to their life span they can enjoy centuries of exploration allowing them to gain greater magical power and will trust in compromise to resolve disputes before violence.

  • Ability score increase: Dexterity +2
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 30ft
  • Size: 4 to 5ft classed as medium size.
  • Dark Vision: Accustomed to life in twilight forests you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions, you can see in dim light within 60ft as if it was clear as day and in darkness as if it was dim.
  • Keen Senses: You are proficient in Perception and gain +2 to perception rolls.
  • Fey Ancestry Gain advantage on saving throws against being charmed, magic can’t put you to sleep.
  • Stress Gain: Low
  • Medium Stress: -1 Insight +1 Perception
  • High Stress: +2 Perception +1 Intellect -2 Strength
  • Stress Action: Their keen sense sharpens further as their body takes on a flight response, they will look for openings to gain distance before attacking and can sense murderous intent within 15ft.

Gnome

“Nothing is busier than a Gnome” is a saying well known through the lands, Gnomes aim to get as much enjoyment out of their three to five countries of life. Gnomish curiosity means they can’t get enough out of the things they want to do and see. To what may seem a carefree existent many Gnomes are skilled engineers, sages, or inventors their willingness to make mistakes and laugh at themselves in the process can lead to taking bold risks.

  • Ability score increase: Intelligence +2
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 25 feet
  • Size: 3 to 4ft classed as small.
  • Dark Vision: Accustomed to life underground you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions, you can see in dim light within 60ft as if it was clear as day and in darkness as if it was dim.
  • Cunning: Gain advantage on all intelligence, wisdom, and charisma saving throws against magic.
  • Stress Gain: High
  • Medium Stress: +2 Wisdom +1 Intelligence -2 Stealth -2 Charisma
  • High Stress:
  • Stress Action: The player must make a saving throw when performing a stealth check to see if their character will panic and start projecting their thoughts aloud notifying any enemy within 10ft. Gnomes will take bold moves to get out of a bad situation gaining advantage on rolls which can have a negative drawback.

Halfing

A kind and curious folk they cherish the bonds of friendship taking enjoyment from the comforts of home while some harbor the dreams of glory or discovery. Halflings are easily moved to pity and hate seeing suffering leading them to be generous sharing what they have even in dire situations. Halflings have survived for many generations living on the edges of wars and politics thanks in hand to their stature avoiding notice and avoiding offense integrating themselves with all walks of life.

  • Ability score increase: Dexterity +2
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 25ft
  • Size: 3ft classed as small.
  • Lucky: When rolling a d20 for an attack, ability, or saving throw you can reroll the dice and must use that new roll.
  • Brave: You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened
  • Nimbleness: You can move through the space of any creature larger than yourself.
  • Stress Gain: Very Low
  • Medium Stress: +1 Acrobatics +1 Charisma -1 Survival -1 Perception
  • High Stress: +2 Acrobatics +1 Dexterity +2 stealth -2 Intelligence
  • Stress Action: Halflings will prioritise the safety of others and will try to distract or gain the attention of an enemy endangering their friends to lure it away, player must roll a d20 on a 12+ the enemy is lured towards them and, can escape from almost any situation and will roll at advantage when escaping an enemy however they will roll at a disadvantage if that means sacrificing an ally.

Half-Elf

Born of human and elf some say they are gifted the best qualities of both parents: Human curiosity, inventiveness, and ambition tempered by their refined senses, love of nature, and artistic taste. Many half-elves considered this to be a double-edged sword as they never truly belong to a single world however they are welcomed into human cities and somewhat less into elven lands. Many half-elves choose to live lives of solidarity wandering or joining with other misfits in the adventuring life.

  • Ability score increase: Charisma +2, +1 in any ability of your choice.
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 30ft
  • Size: 5 to 6ft classed as medium.
  • Dark Vision: Thanks to your elf blood you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions, you can see in dim light within 60ft as if it was clear as day and in darkness as if it was dim.
  • Fae Ancestry: Gain advantage on saving throws against being charmed, magic can’t put you to sleep.
  • Skill Versatility You gain proficiency in two skills of your choice.
  • Stress Gain: Medium / High
  • Medium Stress: -1 History -1 Insight +1 Charisma
  • High Stress: -2 Charisma -1 wisedom +1 Constitution +1 Dexterity +2 Deception
  • Stress Action: Inner insecurities begin to well up increasing your paranoia, making you more susceptible to mental attacks rolling at disadvantage to all perception checks, and gaining advantage on rolls to disengage from enemies.

Half-Orc

Like others half-orcs have the combined nature of both their lineages allowing them to be more accepted in human civilization but that does not come without hardship. From their orcish blood half-orcs typically inherit less desirable qualities of impatience and ill-temper however they also take on the orcish love of physical pleasures such as eating drinking and dancing. Although they are half-human they are unable to fully escape the influence of the one-eyed god Gruumsh where some Half-Orcs hear the whispers of Gruumsh in their dreams calling them to unleash their inner rage.

  • Ability score increase: +2 Strength +1 Constitution
  • Speed: Base walking speed 30ft
  • Size: 5 to 6 1/2ft classed as medium
  • Dark Vision: Thanks to your Orc blood you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions, you can see in dim light within 60ft as if it was clear as day and in darkness as if it was dim.
  • Menacing: You gain proficiency in the Intimidation skill
  • Relentless Endurance: When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit instead. This can’t be used again until your next long rest.
  • Savage Attacks: When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon, you can roll one of the weapon’s dice once more and add it to the extra damage of the crit.
  • Stress Gain: High
  • Medium Stress: +2 Intimidation -1 Intelligence
  • High Stress: +1 Strength +3 Intimidation +1 Athletics -4 Perception -2 Intelligence
  • Stress Action: The Half-Orc enters a fit of panicked rage, they’re immune to immobilization spells or movement impairing effects and gain an additional attack dice. The player cannot truly discern friend from foe in this state and must roll a dice for the number of targets within 10ft. If the target is a friendly player the Half-Orc must roll a perception check to prevent the attack.

Human

The most common of races, short-lived in comparison to most other races they strive to achieve as much as they can in the years gifted. Driving them to become the innovators, achievers, and pioneers of the worlds. Humans are the most adaptable and ambitious among the common races leading them to try to mingle with every race serving as ambassadors, diplomats, and merchants. However, most are self-serving and many of the common races fear they can be easily deceived due to their immaturity.

  • Ability score increase: +1 to each ability score.
  • Speed: Base walking speed is 30ft.
  • Size: 5ft to 6ft classed as medium size
  • Jack of All Trades: You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice.
  • Negotiator: You roll at advantage during any discussion or argument with Non-Playing Characters.
  • Stress Gain: Medium / High
  • Medium Stress: +1 Survival -1 Insight -1 Investigation
  • High Stress: +2 Survival +1 Slieght of Hand +1 Intelligence -1 Constitution -2 Intimidation
  • Stress Action: At high stress, Humans' concentration and conviction wane they roll at disadvantage for all saving rolls against charm, manipulation, and deception. The Human will enter a state of fight or flight the player will roll 1d20 on an 11+ they will enter into a fight response, they will no longer be forced to roll at disadvantage in any attacking situation whilst this is active. On a 10 or lower they will enter flight response they will gain advantage at all avoidance rolls.

Barbarian

Barbarians are defined by rage: Unbridled, unquenchable and unthinking fury. More than a mere emotion anger is the ferocity of a cornered predator. Rage is power than fuels not just a battle frenzy but uncanny reflexs, resilience and feats of strength.

Druid

Druids revere nature above all, ganing spells and alternative magical powers either from the force of nature itself or a nature diety. Druids are an embodiment of nature's resilence cunning and fury. They can call upon the power of tooth and claw, of sun and moon and gain the ability to take on animal forms.

Paladin

Paladins swear an oath to uphold justice and rightouesness, to stand with the good things of the world against the evil that lurks in the darkness. Bound by a sacred oath that grants them their power, a power which increases equally from their diety and a commitment to justice. Paladins train for years to gain mastery of combat but that comes second to the magical power to heal the sick and injured, to smite the wicked and undead.

Ranger

Warriors of the wilderness, ranger specialize in hunting the monsters that threaten the edges of civilization. Their keen predation skills highlighted by the ability to track their prey, gliding stealthily through the wilds. Due to thier affinity with nature rangers aquire the ability to cast spells that harness nature, emphasizing speed and stealth becoming the apex preadators.

Rogue

Rogues rely on skill, stealth and their does' vulnerabilities to get the upper hand in any situation. They are able to find the solution to nearly any problem, using thier intuition, resourcefulness and versatility becoming the linchpin of any party. When it comes to combat a Rogue's prioritizes cunning looking to make precise strikes in critical places to bring an enemy to their final breath.

Sorcerer

Sorcerer's are suffused body, mind and spirit with latent magical power. Sorcerer's can wield magic that springs from their ancient bloodlines, others carry a raw uncontrolled magic within them, leading them to not require spellbooks and ancient tomes that other magic users may rely on, nor a patron as warlocks do. By harnessing their own inborn magic, they can discover new a devestating ways to release it.
